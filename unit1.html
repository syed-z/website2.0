1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?

According to research carried out by computer love, a digital computer studio. An astonishing 97% of companies report to have had digital projects fail. So how in the digital age are we having so many problems with digital technologies. The answer may ironically not be linked to the technology it’s self but how we are organising. Top performing companies attribute their success to a continual improvement process with a strong focus on user needs. Conversely mainstream companies have claimed that a lack of focus on the end user and leaving their testing processes to the end of projects is a big reason for the failure of their projects. The research indicates that mainstream companies have struggled with updating and innovating internal processes that contribute to poor communication between departments, short term priorities taking over, and not clearly defining the vision and strategy.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

With the EU’s data protection laws GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) came into affect as of the 25th of may 2018. These laws brought with them a shift in how companies can handle their customers data. GDPR applies to all businesses and organisations indiscriminately. What this means in practical terms is when considering digital projects the type of data, the way we collect data and what the legal responsibilities are to the users all need to be thought about. There is an emphasis on transparency and structure which inevitably in the long run will have a net positive outcome for your project and business. Businesses will need to adopt the privacy by design framework across all uses and applications. You can find out more about “Privacy By Design Framework” by clicking the link. GDPR will bring greater control for customers over their personal data and is in response to some of the ethical and security issues we now face. Even though we have left the EU, The UK has adopted their own version of GDPR due to Brexit These changes are found in the UK government’s Data Protection, Privacy and Electronic Communications (EU Exit) Regulation (DPPEC regulation).
